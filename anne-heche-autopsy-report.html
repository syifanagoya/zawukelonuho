    What Was Found in Anne Heche’s Autopsy Report?

    A report from the Los Angeles County Department of Medical Examiner-Coroner says that actress Anne Heche was not impaired by any illicit substances when she crashed her car into a house in Los Angeles in August, killing herself from burns and smoke inhalation.

    Anne Heche’s autopsy examination shows the actress was not on drugs at time of fiery car crash https://t.co/E6J3jiN1aX

    — Fox News (@FoxNews) December 7, 2022

    A spokesperson said that Heche’s blood tests at the hospital showed the presence of benzoylecgonine, a substance that is formed when cocaine breaks down but is not active.

    This means that there was no proof that Heche was impaired by drugs at the time of the crash. While cannabinoids were found in her urine, they were not detected in the admission blood and are consistent with prior use, but not at the time of the injury.

    Heche had such bad burns that her body couldn’t absorb air properly, which caused her brain injury from lack of oxygen.

    A representative said that more toxicology results showed that Heche had been given drugs like fentanyl while she was in the hospital getting treatment, but not when she was hurt.
