    Who was Felix Blumenfeld?

    Felix Mikhailovich Blumenfeld was a Russian composer, conductor, pianist, and teacher, known for his contributions to the world of music during his lifetime.

    When and where was Felix Blumenfeld born?

    He was born on April 7, 1863, in Kirovograd, present-day Ukraine, within the Kherson Governorate of the Russian Empire.

    What is he famous for?

    Blumenfeld is renowned for his works as a composer, which include a symphony, numerous pieces for solo piano, an Allegro de Concert for piano and orchestra, and lieder, showcasing influences from Chopin and Tchaikovsky.

    Did Felix Blumenfeld have any notable students?

    Yes, among his famous pupils were Simon Barere, Maria Yudina, Nathan Perelman, Anatole Kitain, and Maria Grinberg.

    Where did he study and teach?

    He studied composition at the St. Petersburg Conservatory under Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov and piano under Fedor Stein between 1881 and 1885. Later, he taught piano there from 1885 until 1918.

    What roles did he hold in his career?

    Besides being a composer and teacher, Blumenfeld served as a conductor of the Imperial Opera St-Petersburg and was in great demand as a piano teacher, pianist, and conductor.

    How did he contribute to the opera world?

    He conducted the Russian premiere of Wagner’s opera Tristan und Isolde and the Paris premiere of Modest Mussorgsky’s opera Boris Godunov.

    What happened to Felix Blumenfeld’s compositions after his death?

    Despite his popularity during his lifetime, Blumenfeld’s compositions were quickly forgotten after his death, although they are recognized for their artistic quality and have seen a renaissance in recent years.

    When did he die?

    He passed away on January 21, 1931, in Moscow, Soviet Union.

    Yes, there has been a renewed appreciation for Blumenfeld’s piano compositions, particularly for teaching and performing, thanks to efforts to create awareness of his works.
