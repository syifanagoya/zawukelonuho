    WWE is going strong toward Survivor Series on November 25th. The Allstate Arena in Chicago will get WarGames, and odds are, Randy Orton will be included on the card.

    Ringside News has comprehensive coverage of WWE RAW this week. As always, keep checking back with our live results for constant updates. You never know what will happen next, especially in WWE.

    This week’s episode of RAW concluded with an Undisputed Universal Tag Team Title match involving the Judgment Day and the team of Cody Rhodes and Jey Uso. However, the outcome did not favor the babyface team.

    During the match, Drew McIntyre made an unexpected appearance at ringside and delivered a punishing Claymore Kick to Jey Uso. This action underlined McIntyre’s ongoing grudge against Jey Uso, stemming from his belief that Jey Uso played a role in preventing him from becoming the WWE Undisputed Universal Champion.

    In the aftermath, Rhea Ripley emerged and exchanged a handshake with Drew McIntyre, confirming their newfound alliance. This development indicates that the Judgment Day now possesses a fifth member for their WarGames team at Survivor Series, aligning with prior reports.

    Cody Rhodes and Jey Uso remained in the ring after WWE RAW went off the air in Washington, DC. The American Nightmare proudly told the Judgment Day that he has friends too. This prompted some big “Randy Orton” chants from the crowd.

    It was previously reported that Randy Orton is expected to be back at Survivor Series as the 5th member of the babyface team. It appears that those plans are moving right along. You can check out the clip below from after WWE RAW this week.
