    Early Life of Bradley Cooper

    On January 5, 1975, Bradley Charles Cooper was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Gloria, his mother, was employed by the neighborhood NBC station. Charles, his father, worked at Merril Lynch as a stockbroker. His mother is from Italy.

    His father is Irish-born. He has a Holly, an elder sister. He was brought up as a Catholic. He enjoyed cooking and playing basketball. He was introduced to movies like “The Elephant Man” by his father, which gave him the desire to pursue acting.

    Cooper attended Germantown Academy and earned a B.A. in English from Georgetown in 1997. He competed on the college rowing squad. In college, Cooper mastered the French language. In 2001, he graduated with a Master of Fine Arts in acting from the Actors Studio Drama School.

    In a supporting role alongside Sarah Jessica Parker on “Sex and the City,” Cooper made his television debut in 1999. Wet Hot American Summer, an ensemble comedy from 2001, served as Cooper’s debut movie. In the 2015 prequel “Wet Hot American Summer: First Day of Camp,” Cooper played the same part again.

    Cooper played Will Tippin, the close friend of Jennifer Garner’s character, in the 2001–2006 television series “Alias.” In the 2005 comedy “Wedding Crashers,” Cooper’s Hollywood career took off. Cooper received the lead part in the Fox television series “Kitchen Confidential,” which is based on Anthony Bourdain’s autobiography, in September 2005.

    The Hangover, a 2009 comedy directed by Todd Phillips, gave Cooper his breakthrough performance. Cooper had an appearance in Garry Marshall’s 2010 ensemble romantic comedy “Valentine’s Day.” Then, in the feature film adaptation of “The A-Team,” he played the fictitious character Templeton Faceman Peck.

    Cooper starred in the suspense film “Limitless” in 2011. He appeared in “The Hangover Part II” that same year. He appeared in several movies in 2012, most notably “Silver Linings Playbook.” He was nominated for an Academy Award, a Golden Globe Award, and a Screen Actors Guild Award for “Silver Linings Playbook” and won an MTV Movie Award for Best Performance.

    Cooper had an appearance in “The Hangover Part III” in 2013. He played an insane FBI agent in “American Hustle” later that year. Cooper co-produced and starred in “American Sniper” in 2014 as US Navy SEAL sniper Chris Kyle. Cooper’s portrayal of Chris Kyle earned him an MTV Movie Award for Best Male Performance. He was also a candidate for another Best Actor Academy Award.

    Cooper made his directorial debut in 2018 with “A Star Is Born,” a remake of the 1937 musical movie of the same name. Cooper acted in the movie as Jackson Maine, an accomplished singer, whose relationship with Ally (played by Lady Gaga) grows difficult as her career starts to eclipse his.

    Cooper was nominated for two Golden Globes—best actor in a drama and best director—and won the National Board of Review Award for best director for the movie. For “Shallow,” Gaga and Cooper received two Grammy nominations: Best Pop Duo/Group Performance and Record of the Year. The Grammy for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance went to Cooper and Gaga. The three Academy Awards for which Cooper was nominated were for Best Picture, Best Actor, and Best Adapted Screenplay.
